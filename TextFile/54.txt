Muara Bungo - Wikipedia Muara Bungo From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Jump to navigation Jump to search A park in Muara Bungo Muara Bungo is a town in the Indonesian province of Jambi, Sumatra. It is the capital of Bungo Regency,[1][2] a regency (kabupaten) in Sumatra. It had a population of 21,243 as of the 2020 census.[3] Contents 1 Geography 1.1 Waterways 2 Climate 3 Infrastructure 3.1 Transportation 3.2 Emergency services 4 Business and commerce 5 Land use 6 Schools 7 Crime 8 References 9 Further reading Geography[edit] Waterways[edit] The Benit river runs through Muara Bungo, and proven gold deposits in the river within Muara Bungo were estimated at 1,383 kilograms (3,049 lb) in 2005.[4] Climate[edit] Muara Bungo has a tropical rainforest climate (Af) with heavy rainfall year-round. The damage estimate was mostly attributed to building fires.[10] Muara Bungo also has a police department.[11][12] Business and commerce[edit] A branch of Bank Rakyat Indonesia is located in Muara Bungo.[12] Land use[edit] Natural rubber is produced in Muara Bungo,[13] and rubber farming is a primary source of income and livelihood for the majority of the city's residents.[14] An agroforestry innovation involved the planting of rubber trees and cinnamon alongside one-another to diversify plantings to avoid monoculture.[15] Muara Bungo has some large oil palm plantations,[16] and palm oil is produced there.[17] Circa 1995 to 1996, illegal timber harvesting was observed in Muara Bungo.[18] Schools[edit] The University of Muara Bungo is located in the city.[19] Crime[edit] In June 2015, it was reported that street crime rates were increasing in Muara Bungo.[14] Crimes have included mugging (theft by violence), auto theft and robberies.[14] It was reported by Tribun Jambi that a majority of the crimes were committed by outsiders who do not reside in Muara Bungo.[14] References[edit] ^ "Runway Bandara Muara Bungo Bakal Jadi 2.000 Meter".
