She wrote books about the convicts transported to Australia. Contents 1 Early life 2 Career 3 Honours and recognition 4 Death 5 Publications 5.1 Non-fiction 5.2 Novel 5.3 Chapter contribution 6 References 7 External links Early life[edit] Born Babette Alison Macfarlan on 2 April 1942,[1] Smith was educated at Frensham, Mittagong, New South Wales (NSW).[2] She completed a BA/LLB dual-degree program at the University of Sydney.[3][4] She began work at the Elizabethan Theatre Trust, before joining Harry M. Miller, working on productions including Hair.[5] In the 1970s, Smith worked as a television producer at Channel 9 in Sydney[6] and as publicist for films, including Silver City.[7] Then, she followed up with a stint at Hoyts as national marketing manager.[4] Career[edit] Her first book, A Cargo of Women, grew from her discovery that her great-great-grandmother, Susannah Watson, was a convict transported to Australia in 1829.[8] Smith served as chief executive of the NSW Bar Association from 1993 to 1997,[9] the first woman to do so.[10] She worked as a mediator, including with Corrective Services NSW in Silverwater and Lithgow jails.[10] She was a member of the Australian Republican Movement and served on its NSW Committee for two years.[9] She was invited to present the 2009 Russel Ward Annual Lecture at the University of New England.[11] The Mitchell Library at the State Library of New South Wales holds two collections of her papers (25 boxes) covering her research, writing and employment.[9][12] Jeannine Baker interviewed Smith in 2018, with the recording held by the National Film and Sound Archive.[5] Honours and recognition[edit] Smith was awarded the Medal of the Order of Australia in the 2005 Queen's Birthday Honours for "service to the community, particularly as an historian and author".[13] The Luck of the Irish won the 2015 NSW Premier's Prize for Regional and Community history and was shortlisted for the Australian Historical Association’s Kay Daniels award for excellence in research in 2016.[14][15] Death[edit] Smith died on 22 November 2021.[3] She was survived by her son, Joshua.[1] Publications[edit] Non-fiction[edit] A Cargo of Women: Susannah Watson and the Convicts of the Princess Royal, NSW University Press, 1988, ISBN 978-0-86840-067-9;
